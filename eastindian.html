States of East India

West Bengal

West Bengal culture is a rich blend of artistic, literary, and spiritual traditions, famous for its vibrant festivals like Durga Puja, renowned literature (Tagore), cinema (Satyajit Ray), folk music (Baul, Jatra), and exquisite handicrafts, showcasing a unique Bengali identity rooted in creativity and intellectualism.

Odisha

Odisha's culture is a vibrant mix of ancient traditions, spiritual devotion (especially to Lord Jagannath), and artistic expression, seen in its intricate temples (Konark, Lingaraj), classical Odissi dance, Pattachitra paintings, and numerous festivals like Ratha Yatra, blending Aryan, Dravidian, and tribal influences.

Bihar

Bihar's culture is a vibrant mix of ancient history, spirituality (Buddhism, Jainism), and folk traditions, known for distinct art like Madhubani painting, unique cuisine (Litti Chokha), famous festivals (Chhath Puja), and rich literature, reflecting simple living and communal harmony.

Jharkhand

Jharkhand's culture is a vibrant blend of diverse tribal traditions (Santhal, Munda, Oraon) and local influences, celebrated through nature-inspired art like Paitkar painting, Tussar silk, and rituals honoring flora and fauna, with festivals like Karam, Sohrai, and Chhau dance showcasing deep connection to the land.
